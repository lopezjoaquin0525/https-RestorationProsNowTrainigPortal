  • 6. SDR Script 
    SDR Script

Invitation:
Hello. Good morning. I was calling for your restoration property services. (Pause)
Yes, we're looking for someone to connect with our residential and commercial clients.
Would you be the person I could speak with? (Pause, listen to their response & assess decision-making authority)

Prospect Responses:
Decision Maker: "Yes, I'm the (Founder / Owner / Manager)" or "How may I help you?"
Gatekeeper: Do you have an active job? Do you need service? Do you have an emergency? Is this for water, fire, or mold?
Who are you? What company are you from? What is the purpose of the call?

Pitch:
We're connecting one or two trusted restoration service providers in the area with our clients who need help with their property damage.
As part of our launch, you'll receive up to 5 active work jobs over the next 30 to 60 days with no fees, costs, or percentages—
so you can see how we operate. All we ask in return is a testimonial based on your experience.

Qualifying Questions:
1) How long have you been in business, and what types of verticals do you prefer — water damage, mold remediation, hotels, apartments, senior living?
2) Do you service from one location or multiple?
3) What areas or ZIP codes would you prefer to receive this active work?
4) Do you handle mold-related jobs? If so, do you have licensing?
5) What's your preferred alert method — text, email, or phone call?
6) How quickly are you typically able to respond or dispatch? [Do you offer 24/7 emergency services?]

Close:
Thanks for sharing — sounds great. Let's set up a quick 20–30 minute Zoom meeting with our founder, Erik Lundgaard.
He'll walk you through the important details and answer all your questions.
Would [10:00 AM Central Time] work for you? And what's the best email address to send your private Calendly invite?
Perfect — just to confirm, you're booked for [Tuesday, June 17th at 10:00 AM Central Time].
If the Calendly link shows a different time zone, no worries — it's still set for [10:00 AM Central].

Appointment Setting Workflow

  • Initial call using script
  • Qualify lead (location, service type, license, insurance)
  • Schedule via Calendly and confirm email/phone
  • Follow-up 24 hours prior to appointment
  • Document lead in CRM with full details and tags
  • Notify contractor of upcoming appointment

    Intake Script

• Client's emotional tone
• Urgency or pain points

"Just to clarify — we're the emergency dispatch team, not the contractor. We connect you with a licensed, insured, and vetted contractor who will provide:

Transfer Protocol
Pre-Transfer (Client):
"I'm going to connect you now with [Contractor's Name] from [Company Name].
They are [water damage experts] and should arrive within 30–60 minutes. Let's get them on the line!"

Prep the Contractor (Before Live Transfer):
"Hey [Contractor Name], it's [Your Name] at Restoration Pros Now. Got a new lead for you:
Client: [Client Name]
Location: [City]
Type of Damage: [e.g., Category 2 water, mold, etc.]
Cause: [e.g., pipe burst]
Insurance: [Yes/No]
Connecting you now."

Live Introduction (3-way call):
"[Client Name], I've got [Contractor Name] on the call from [Company Name].
They'll take it from here and take care of your (type of emergency property damage.)" 

Important: Mute and drop only after both parties hang up.

Warm Transfer Workflow

Step-by-step warm transfer process:

  • Gather emergency details and client information (intake complete)
  • Reassure client: "I'll connect you directly with our licensed contractor."
  • Pre-brief contractor with:
    • Client name and location
    • Type and category of emergency
    • Cause (if known)
    • Insurance status
  • Initiate 3-way call: introduce client to contractor
  • Hand off by saying: "[Contractor], the call is yours to assist [Client]."
  • Mute and remain on call until both parties confirm connection, then drop off
